The Complete Terpenes Guide

The aromatic science behind your cannabis experience

What Are Terpenes?

Terpenes are aromatic compounds found throughout the plant kingdom. Cannabis produces over 150 different terpenes in its trichome glands. They create each strain's distinctive smell and flavor, but they also actively shape the effects you feel through the entourage effect -- a synergistic interaction between cannabinoids and terpenes.

Major Terpenes

Myrcene

Smell: Earthy, musky, fruity. Effects: Sedating, relaxing, "couch-lock." May help with: Pain, sleep, inflammation. Found in: Mangoes, hops, lemongrass. Research suggests myrcene may help cannabinoids cross the blood-brain barrier more efficiently.

Limonene

Smell: Bright citrus. Effects: Uplifting, energizing, mood-elevating. May help with: Anxiety, depression, stress. Found in: Citrus rinds, juniper, rosemary.

Linalool

Smell: Floral lavender with spicy notes. Effects: Calming, sedating. May help with: Anxiety, insomnia, pain. Works on GABA receptors like anti-anxiety medications. Found in: Lavender, birch bark, coriander.

Pinene

Smell: Fresh pine and forest. Effects: Alertness, mental clarity. May help with: Inflammation, memory. May counteract THC's memory impairment. Found in: Pine trees, rosemary, basil.

Beta-Caryophyllene

Smell: Peppery, spicy, woody. Effects: Anti-inflammatory, pain-relieving. Unique: Actually binds to CB2 receptors like a cannabinoid -- the only terpene known to do this. Found in: Black pepper, cloves, cinnamon.

Humulene

Smell: Hoppy, earthy. Effects: Anti-inflammatory, appetite suppression. Unlike most cannabis compounds, humulene may reduce appetite. Found in: Hops, coriander, basil.

The Entourage Effect

Cannabis compounds work better together than alone. Like a band -- the full song is greater than any solo. This is why full-spectrum products (all natural compounds) are often preferred over isolates (single purified compound). Broad-spectrum products offer a middle ground with THC removed.

Terpene Tips

1. Smell before you buy -- your nose knows. 2. Terpenes absorb best via inhalation or sublingually. 3. Store in airtight containers, cool and dark. 4. Look for lab tests with terpene profiles. 5. Match terpenes to needs: myrcene+linalool for relaxation, limonene+pinene for alertness.

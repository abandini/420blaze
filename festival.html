We're Building This Festival For You

Cleveland's 420 scene is growing — but there's nothing like a proper all-day festival. Every event this year sold out instantly or started too late. So we're planning something better for 2027, and we want your input on every detail.

This isn't a corporate event. This is Blazin Bill and the 420Blazin community building Cleveland's 420 festival from scratch. Your answers below directly shape what this becomes.

The Vision

  • Wake & Bake Brunch (10 AM): Start the day right. Coffee bar, mimosas, breakfast, and mellow morning vibes.
  • Afternoon Sessions (12–5 PM): Reggae, jam bands, vendor village, dispensary booths, glass art, food trucks, and gear demos.
  • Evening Headliners (5 PM–12 AM): Funk, soul, and psychedelic rock building to the finale.
  • The Closer: A Cleveland tradition reborn — Maggot Brain at midnight. WMMS played the full Funkadelic cut every Sunday at 1:30 AM from 1976 to 1995. We're bringing it back, live, to close the night.

Help Us Pick the Venue

We're considering three very different venues. Each creates a different festival experience. Which one speaks to you?

The Agora Theatre

The Big-League Option
Up to 2,000 • 5000 Euclid Ave

Cleveland's legendary rock venue. Full production, two rooms (brunch in the Ballroom, main acts in the Theatre). Parliament-Funkadelic, the Dead — everyone has played this stage. Professional sound and lights included.

Cuyahoga County Fairgrounds

The Space & Value Option
2,500+ • Middleburg Heights

Massive indoor space across multiple buildings. The Cannabis & Music Expo already uses this venue. Tons of room for vendors, food trucks, and activities. 7,500 parking spots. Budget-friendly means cheaper tickets for everyone.

Forest City Brewery

The Intimate Craft Experience
Up to 250 • 2135 Columbus Rd

Historic Waldorf Beer Hall with cavernous ceilings, warm woodwork, craft beer on tap, and a beer garden. Intimate, up-close vibes. Smaller crowd means you're part of the show, not watching from a distance.

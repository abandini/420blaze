## VENUE OPTIONS (Ranked)

### Tier 1 — Purpose-Built Music Venues

#### 1. Agora Theatre (RECOMMENDED)
- **Address:** 5000 Euclid Ave, Cleveland, OH 44103
- **Capacity:** Theatre: 2,000 GA / 1,100 seated | Ballroom: 500 GA
- **Rental:** Theatre $8,500 + expenses | Ballroom $6,500 + expenses
- **Includes:** House sound, house lights, stage, security, event management, concessions, ticketing/box office, talent booking assistance
- **Why it fits:** Cleveland's iconic rock venue. Full production infrastructure. Can run both rooms simultaneously (brunch in Ballroom, main acts in Theatre). Historic — Parliament-Funkadelic, Dead, everybody has played here.
- **Contact:** Brooklyn DiFranco, bdifranco@aegpresents.com | (216) 881-2221
- **Considerations:** AEG-managed — may have exclusivity requirements for ticketing (Ticketmaster). Alcohol through their concessions. All-day (10am-midnight) would need custom negotiation.

## FOOD & BEVERAGE

### Food Trucks (Recommended)
- **Cost:** Food trucks are FREE to you — they pay YOU a percentage (10-20% of sales) or a flat pitch fee ($200-$500/truck)
- **Per-person spend:** $15–$30 per guest on food
- **Recommendation:** 4-6 food trucks for 500-1000 attendees
- **Cleveland Food Truck Association:** clefta.org — single point of contact for booking multiple trucks
- **Book 6+ months ahead** for April dates
- **Revenue potential:** 4 trucks × $300 pitch fee = $1,200, or 15% of sales on 800 attendees × $20 avg = $2,400

### Bar/Alcohol
- At Agora/HoB: handled by venue (they keep bar revenue or negotiate split)
- At Fairgrounds/78th: need licensed caterer or temp liquor permit from Ohio DOLC
- Ohio temporary liquor permit: ~$50/day for non-profit, more complex for for-profit events
- **Revenue potential:** $8–$15 per person in drinks × 800 attendees = $6,400–$12,000

### "Wake & Bake Brunch" Menu Concept
- Partner with a local restaurant or catering company
- Brunch ticket add-on: $25–$35/person
- Menu: eggs, waffles, bacon, coffee bar, mimosas, Bloody Marys
- Infused options depend on Ohio cannabis consumption laws at events

## LEGAL & PERMITS

- **Ohio temporary event permit** — contact local municipality
- **Liquor permit** — Ohio DOLC temporary permit required if self-serving alcohol
- **Fire marshal inspection** — required for venues over 300 capacity
- **Event liability insurance** — $1M–$2M policy, ~$2,000–$5,000
- **Cannabis consumption:** Ohio law restricts public consumption. Private venue with designated areas may be possible — consult Ohio cannabis attorney. The Cannabis Expo navigates this successfully.
- **Music licensing:** ASCAP/BMI/SESAC blanket license. Many venues (Agora, HoB) already have these.
- **Food handler permits** — handled by food trucks (they carry their own)

The sold-out Beachland and Winchester events this weekend prove Cleveland has 420 event demand with zero supply at scale. Nobody is doing a proper all-day festival. The Cannabis & Music Expo is the closest thing, and it's just a vendor expo with music — not a curated festival experience with a narrative arc (brunch → afternoon → evening → midnight closer).

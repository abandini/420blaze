## 1. Brand Positioning & Differentiation

### Where 420Blazin Sits

**Position:** Premium-accessible cannabis streetwear — above Redbubble/Etsy commodity, below Cookies SF premium.

| Tier | Brands | Tee Price | 420Blazin |
|------|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Commodity | Redbubble, Amazon generic | $15-22 | - |
| **420Blazin tier** | StonerDays, Etsy creators | **$24.99-34.99** | **Here** |
| Premium streetwear | Cookies SF, HUF | $33-46 | Aspirational |
| Luxury | Binske, Gucci collabs | $100+ | Not competing |

### Core Differentiators

1. **The "Blaze" concept** — Fire/flame energy that most cannabis brands don't own. Warm palettes (amber, burnt orange, flame red + black) instead of the predictable greens everyone else uses.
2. **AI-generated art with human curation** — Unique, unreplicable designs at scale. The hybrid workflow (AI art + manual typography) is both a production advantage and a copyright strategy.
3. **Multi-aesthetic catalog** — Serve ALL personas, not just one lane. Every design in the store should clearly map to a persona.
4. **Limited drop mentality** — Even in POD, create urgency with seasonal/numbered designs.

### Strategic Imperative: Develop Core Symbols

The Grateful Dead built a $4.4B licensing empire on 5 simple, remixable symbols. 420Blazin needs 3-5 signature icons:

| Symbol | Role | Usage |
|--------|------|-------|
| Custom wordmark | Primary brand identity | Every product, all placements |
| Mascot character | Remixable across all products | Like The Hundreds' Adam Bomb |
| Signature icon | Quick-read brand signal | Hats, small placements, embroidery |
| Pattern motif | All-over prints, textiles | Socks, AOP hoodies, accessories |
| "420" treatment | Stylized number mark | Limited editions, date-specific drops |

## 2. Competitor Intelligence

### Cookies SF — The Benchmark

- **Revenue:** ~$3.1M/6mo e-commerce; $500M/yr total (cannabis + merch + retail)
- **Design DNA:** Bold logo-forward. Custom script wordmark with integrated leaf. Signature "Cookies Blue" (royal blue) — a NON-green signature color that differentiates
- **Best sellers:** Original Thin Mint Black Tee (logo-only), Infamous Logo Tee, hoodies, fitted caps
- **Pricing:** Tees $36-40, Hoodies $85-100, Hats $38-42, Jackets ~$210
- **Why it works:** Authenticity. Cookies is a REAL cannabis brand ($500M/yr) — the merch is the lifestyle extension. Berner's celebrity status drives aspirational pull
- **POD lesson:** You can't replicate the "real brand" authenticity, but you CAN replicate bold logo-forward design in a signature brand color

### HUF — The Crossover King

- **Design DNA:** Clean, minimal, blocky typography. The "Plantlife" leaf is a tessellated repeating pattern, NOT a single big leaf — textile approach
- **Best seller:** Plantlife Socks ($14/pair) — the iconic gateway product since ~2005
- **Product range:** Socks → base layers ($90), sweaters ($110), fleece ($60-105), jewelry ($22-85)
- **Pricing:** Socks $14, Tees $30-46, Hoodies $80-110
- **Why it works:** Subtle identity signaling — recognizable to insiders, reads as "cool pattern" to outsiders. Seasonal colorways create collectibility
- **POD lesson:** All-over repeating cannabis patterns are HUGELY translatable to POD. Make cannabis imagery into a textile pattern, not a centered graphic. Socks are an incredible gateway product

### Grateful Dead — The $4.4B Blueprint

- **5 iconic elements:** Steal Your Face skull, Dancing Bears, Skull & Roses (Bertha), Tie-Dye, Terrapin Turtle
- **Design DNA:** Hand-drawn illustration quality. Crowd-sourced from "lot culture" fans. Imagery transcends the music — you don't need to be a Deadhead to want the merch
- **Pricing:** Liquid Blue tees $32-34, Official $30-45, Nike SB Dunk collab $110 retail / $500+ resale
- **Why it works:** ICONIC + SIMPLE + REPEATABLE symbols that remix endlessly. Multi-generational appeal. Tie-dye is inherently eye-catching
- **POD lesson:** The most important case study. Simple, remixable symbols beat complex illustrations. Tie-dye + cannabis is an evergreen combination

### PLEASURES — The Dark Edge

- **Design DNA:** Dark, edgy, provocative. Punk/grunge/hardcore aesthetic. Album art and concert flyer references. Underground zine typography
- **Pricing:** Tees $46-55, Hoodies $90-200, Jackets to $240
- **Why it works:** "I am not mainstream" identity signal. Celebrity co-signs (Playboi Carti, The Weeknd). Discomfort and edge sell when design quality is high
- **POD lesson:** There's a market for DARK/EDGY cannabis merch. Vintage reefer madness propaganda reimagined, anti-prohibition punk imagery, countercultural statements

### Stüssy / LRG / The Hundreds — Design DNA

| Brand | Key Insight for 420Blazin |
|-------|--------------------------|
| **Stüssy** | Hand-drawn script logo = authentic and premium. A custom hand-lettered wordmark carries more weight than any font |
| **LRG** | "Hustle Trees" uses a TREE instead of a leaf — recognizable to insiders, plausibly deniable to outsiders |
| **The Hundreds** | A MASCOT CHARACTER (Adam Bomb) is enormously valuable — works on everything from tees to pins |

### StonerDays — The POD Native

- **Founded:** 2012, largest US producer of 420 gear
- **Approach:** Bold pop-culture references, tie-dye, seasonal drops, dab mats
- **Pricing:** Tees $29.99, Hoodies $59.99, Crop tops $49.99
- **Why it works:** Massive catalog with consistent new releases. Mix of "obvious stoner" and subtle designs. Community-first (92.2K Instagram)

### POD Marketplace (Etsy/Redbubble) — What Actually Sells

**Top-performing categories ranked by volume:**
1. **Humor/pun text** — "I'd Hit That," "Wake & Bake," "I Took My Meds Today"
2. **Retro/vintage propaganda** — Reefer madness reimagined. GROWING FASTEST
3. **Psychedelic/trippy art** — Cosmic cannabis, kaleidoscopic patterns
4. **Minimalist/subtle** — Small icons, simple text, work-appropriate
5. **Pop culture mashups** — "Stoner Things," movie/TV parodies (legal risk)
6. **Strain-specific** — OG Kush, Gorilla Glue designs for connoisseurs

## 3. Buyer Personas

### Persona 1: "The Culture Repper" (Primary Target)

- **Who:** Male, 21-34, urban, $35K-65K income
- **Buys:** Graphic tees, snapbacks, hoodies with bold cannabis imagery
- **Design taste:** Bold logos, streetwear, urban colorways (black, purple, green, gold)
- **Price:** Will pay $30-40 tees, $60-80 hoodies if design is fire
- **Trigger:** Instagram/TikTok impulse. 60% FOMO-driven within 24 hours. Needs social proof
- **Design for them:** Streetwear graffiti, bold logos, Cookies SF energy

### Persona 2: "The Canna-Wellness Woman"

- **Who:** Female, 25-40, suburban/urban, $50K-90K income
- **Buys:** Fitted tees, tanks, cozy hoodies, tote bags. Subtle/artistic cannabis references
- **Design taste:** Minimalist, botanical, muted earth tones, sage green. "Elevated not stoner." Wearable to brunch
- **Price:** $28-35 tees, $55-75 hoodies. Values quality and eco-friendly positioning
- **Trigger:** Planned purchases. Instagram lifestyle content, Pinterest. Gift-buyer
- **Design for them:** Cottagecore botanical, wellness zen, subtle line art

### Persona 3: "The Connoisseur"

- **Who:** 28-45, any gender, $60K-120K, legal states
- **Buys:** Premium hoodies, AOP pieces, accessories. Strain-specific or science designs. Limited editions
- **Design taste:** Sophisticated cannabis art, terpene profiles, trichome illustrations. Luxury aesthetics — gold, elegant typography
- **Price:** $35-45 tees, $70-100 AOP hoodies. Responds to "limited edition" and numbered runs (will pay 3x more)
- **Trigger:** Planned + impulse on drops. Active in communities. Collects pieces
- **Design for them:** Sacred geometry, premium botanical, artist collaborations

### Persona 4: "The Gift Giver"

- **Who:** Any gender, 25-55, $40K-80K. Not necessarily a heavy user
- **Buys:** Entry-price: stickers ($3-6), phone cases, mugs, funny tees. "Conversation starter" designs
- **Design taste:** Funny, punny, clever wordplay. Pop culture mashups. Giftable (not crude)
- **Price:** HIGH sensitivity. Sweet spot $15-30 per item. Responds to bundles
- **Trigger:** Seasonal — 420 (April), Christmas (46% of top sales days Nov-Dec), birthdays, Valentine's
- **Design for them:** Humor/puns, clever wordplay, pop culture mashups

### Persona 5: "The Festival Goer"

- **Who:** 21-30, any gender, $25K-55K
- **Buys:** Statement tees, tie-dye, AOP, bucket hats. Eye-catching and Instagram-worthy
- **Design taste:** Psychedelic, trippy, retro 70s, vibrant, maximalist. "Instagrammable" impact
- **Price:** Medium-high sensitivity. Tees $22-28. Very responsive to "420 sale" discounts
- **Trigger:** Impulse pre-event. TikTok/Instagram discovery. Countdown timers and "limited run" messaging
- **Design for them:** Psychedelic art, tie-dye, festival graphics, all-over prints

### Persona 6: "The OG / Legacy Smoker"

- **Who:** Male, 40-60, $50K-100K, 20+ years smoking
- **Buys:** Classic tees, comfortable hoodies, vintage-style pieces, rolling trays, mugs
- **Design taste:** Retro/vintage, 70s/80s throwback, Cheech & Chong era. Classic leaf done well. Simple, "OG" vibes
- **Price:** $25-35 tees, $50-70 hoodies. Value-conscious but has income
- **Trigger:** Search/Google/email, not social media. Loyal once they find a brand
- **Design for them:** Vintage propaganda, retro 70s, heritage designs

### Persona 7: "The Micro-Doser / Quiet Consumer"

- **Who:** Any gender, 28-45, $60K-100K+, professional/white-collar
- **Buys:** "IYKYK" designs — subtle, only fellow consumers would recognize. Premium basics with quiet cannabis nods
- **Design taste:** Ultra-minimalist. Small embroidered details, abstract botanical, clever typography. "You'd never know it's a weed shirt"
- **Price:** LOW sensitivity. $30-40 tees, $65-85 hoodies. Values fabric and fit
- **Trigger:** Word-of-mouth, curated content. Buys direct from brands they respect
- **Design for them:** Minimalist line art, small icons, tone-on-tone, abstract

### Critical Insight: The Gender Gap

Cannabis consumers are now **~50/50 gender split** (51% male, 48.5% female). But most merch brands still design 80%+ for men. **This is the underserved gap.** 420Blazin should explicitly design 40-50% of catalog for female consumers or gender-neutral.

## 4. Design System

### What Makes 420 Merch Actually Cool

**DO:**
- Bold, oversized graphics that fill the chest/canvas
- Art + text combos — visual storytelling, not just text or just imagery
- Cannabis leaf INTEGRATED into the design, not slapped on
- Limited high-contrast palettes (2-3 colors max + metallic accent)
- Graffiti/bubble/block/gothic letterforms with personality
- Retro 70s psychedelic OR modern streetwear edge (pick a lane per design)
- Smoke effects, glow effects, distressed textures
- Cultural identity signaling — designs people are PROUD to wear
- Reference premium brands: Cookies SF, HUF, Dead aesthetic
- Fire/flame elements that own the "Blazin" brand identity

**DON'T:**
- Plain text in sans-serif fonts (Arial, Helvetica)
- Tiny logos in huge transparent canvases
- Generic clipart cannabis leaves
- Low-contrast single-color designs
- Designs that don't fill the print area
- Over-detailed illustrations that look muddy at print size
- Neon "dispensary green" (#00FF00) — signals low-end
- Same design on every product without adaptation
- Ignore the subtlety spectrum — not everyone wants a loud weed shirt

## 5. Color Psychology

### Why Greens/Golds/Blacks Dominate

| Color | Signal | Use Case |
|-------|--------|----------|
| **Green** (sage/forest/olive) | Growth, wellness, nature. Premium greens ONLY | Core brand palette, botanical designs |
| **Black** | Sophistication, edge, universal base | Best garment color for graphic prints |
| **Gold** | Premium, luxury, heritage | Accents, foil effects, limited editions |
| **Purple** | Luxury, creativity, indica vibes | Underused, high differentiation potential |
| **Amber/Burnt Orange** | Fire, "Blazin" brand energy | 420Blazin differentiator — most brands avoid warm tones |
| **Tie-dye rainbow** | Psychedelic, Dead DNA, festival | Always works. Extremely POD-friendly |
| **Earth tones** (terracotta, olive, brown) | Organic, artisanal, premium | Growing trend for elevated cannabis |
| **Pastels** (lavender, mint, soft pink) | Wellness, calm, feminine-forward | Women 25-40, wellness consumers |

### Non-Traditional Palettes — Differentiation Opportunities

| Palette | Target | Mood |
|---------|--------|------|
| Matte black + single pop (orange, teal) | Design-conscious 25-40 | Modern minimalism |
| Cream/off-white + forest green | Premium 30+ | Heritage, craft, artisan |
| Neon gradients (pink/cyan/purple) | Gen Z, festival | Y2K/vaporwave energy |
| Deep purple + cosmic blue + gold | Psychonaut community | Spiritual, metaphysical |

### The Three-Color Rule

Best-performing cannabis brands limit to **3 colors maximum**: primary + secondary accent + neutral. Two colors + metallic accent is the sweet spot. This discipline is essential for POD — print clarity degrades with complex palettes.

### 420Blazin Signature Palette

**Primary:** Amber/burnt orange (owns the "Blaze") + black
**Secondary:** Gold accents for premium signaling
**Seasonal rotation:** Greens, purples, tie-dye for variety — but amber/black is always the anchor

## 6. Typography

### What Works for Cannabis Brands

| Style | Signal | Best For | Notes |
|-------|--------|----------|-------|
| **Neo-Gothic / Blackletter** | Heritage, edge, tattoo | Streetwear, hardcore market | Top trend 2025-2026. Sharp edges, ornate details. Great on hoodies/hats |
| **Graffiti / Street Art** | Youth, rebellion, urban | Gen Z, hip-hop crossover | Bold, colorful. Perceived youthful and unique |
| **Bubble Letters** | Playful, friendly, nostalgic | Fun brands, stickers | 90s nostalgia. Chunky rounded shapes |
| **Oversized Statement** | Bold identity, streetwear | Graphic tees, hoodies | Type AS the primary design element. Strong 2025-2026 trend |
| **Handwritten / Script** | Personal, authentic, artisanal | Limited runs, artist collabs | Signals authenticity in AI-sameness era |
| **Modern Sans-Serif** | Clean, wellness, professional | Wellness positioning | For the "not a stoner" consumer |
| **Revival Serif** | Luxury, heritage, craft | Premium positioning | Signals sophistication |

### Typography Rules

- Maximum 3 fonts per design (stylized headline + clean body + accent)
- Hand-drawn script logos read as authentic (Stüssy model)
- Blocky clean sans-serifs read as streetwear (HUF model)
- Punk/DIY typewriter fonts read as counterculture (PLEASURES model)
- AI generators CANNOT reliably render text — always add typography manually in post-production

### 420Blazin Typography

**Primary logotype:** Custom blackletter or hand-lettered (signals the "blaze" fire energy)
**Secondary:** Clean sans-serif for body/descriptions
**Seasonal rotation:** Graffiti, bubble, handwritten for variety while keeping core mark consistent

## 7. Illustration Styles That Sell

### Ranked by Commercial Performance

**Tier 1: Highest Volume**
- **Statement typography** — Clean text designs. #1 POD seller. "I Took My Meds Today" = top Amazon cannabis tee. Humor + readable typography = broadest appeal
- **Pop culture mashups** — Cannabis leaf integrated into recognizable cultural references. StonerDays' entire brand model. (Caution: IP risk on direct parodies)

**Tier 2: Strong Performers**
- **Psychedelic / neo-psychedelia** — Vivid swirls, kaleidoscopic patterns, dreamlike visuals. Strong festival market. Works exceptionally on AOP
- **Minimalist line art** — Single-weight line illustrations. The "Apple aesthetic" of cannabis. Excellent print clarity
- **Hand-drawn / tattoo flash** — Chunky lines, visible imperfections, artisanal feel. Strong on premium drops
- **Retro/vintage propaganda** — Reefer madness reimagined. FASTEST GROWING category. Underserved goldmine

**Tier 3: Niche but Growing**
- **Anime/manga crossover** — Original characters in anime style with cannabis elements. Larger market than expected
- **3D / inflated graphics** — Chrome, dimensional letterforms. Eye-catching in feeds
- **Sacred geometry / mandala** — Intricate patterns with embedded cannabis/mushroom elements. Spiritual demo

### POD Execution Notes

- Fewer colors + high contrast = cleanest prints
- DTG handles complex art better than screen printing
- Die-cut sticker shapes dramatically outperform rectangles
- Embroidery = 6-8 colors max, bold shapes only, no fine detail
- Designs under 12"x16" for standard tee print area

## 8. Cannabis Leaf Integration

### The Problem

The raw cannabis leaf signals "amateur" or "low-end" to sophisticated consumers. Most premium brands now avoid literal leaf imagery unless used subtly.

### Premium Integration Techniques

| Technique | Description | POD Application |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Negative space** | Leaf shape revealed through absence, hidden in composition | Requires careful design, high impact |
| **Geometric abstraction** | Leaf reduced to triangles/geometry. Insider-recognizable | Clean prints, embroidery-friendly |
| **Botanical illustration** | Scientific drawing style — "plant" not "drug" | Totes, wellness line, stickers |
| **Repeating pattern** | Small-scale tessellation creating texture (HUF Plantlife) | AOP prints, socks, accessories |
| **Deconstructed** | Individual leaflets separated, used as independent elements | Versatile across all products |
| **Material expression** | Tone-on-tone, embossing, watermark effect | Premium feel, subtle signaling |
| **Cultural mashup** | Leaf integrated into skull, mandala, flag, other symbol | The Skull & Roses approach — proven seller |
| **Flame fusion** | Leaf tips that transition into flames (420Blazin specific) | Owns the brand identity |

### 420Blazin Approach

Given the "Blazin" name: **a leaf that incorporates flame elements** — leaf tips transitioning into flames, or a leaf shape constructed from fire/smoke imagery. This owns the symbol while making it distinctly ours.

## 9. Cultural Aesthetic Lanes

### A. Grateful Dead / Jam Band
- **Visual:** Tie-dye, dancing bears, skulls with roses, lightning bolts, rainbow psychedelic
- **Target:** 35-55, festival culture, higher disposable income
- **POD execution:** All-over tie-dye, stickers, tapestries. Create ORIGINAL icons (not Dead IP) in this visual language
- **420Blazin products:** Tie-dye tees with skull & roses, festival hoodies

### B. Hip-Hop / Streetwear
- **Visual:** Bold logos, oversized graphics, dark palettes with pop colors, graffiti, chain motifs
- **Target:** 18-35, urban, music-driven, status-conscious
- **POD execution:** Oversized back prints on hoodies, embroidered hats, bold graphic tees
- **420Blazin products:** Logo tees, snapbacks, brand-forward hoodies

### C. Wellness / Zen
- **Visual:** Soft colors (sage, lavender, cream), clean lines, botanical illustration, yoga/meditation
- **Target:** 25-45, women-skewing, health-conscious, newer to cannabis
- **POD execution:** Muted palettes, simple line art. Should feel like a wellness brand that includes cannabis
- **420Blazin products:** Botanical tees, tote bags, fitted tanks

### D. Psychedelic Art
- **Visual:** Fractals, optical illusions, melting landscapes, impossible geometry, DMT-inspired, Alex Grey
- **Target:** 20-40, psychonaut, festival/rave, art collectors
- **POD execution:** AOP is ESSENTIAL — psychedelic doesn't work as small chest print. Tapestries, hoodies, stickers
- **420Blazin products:** AOP hoodies, all-over tees, large stickers

### E. Tattoo Art
- **Visual:** American traditional bold outlines, neo-traditional, Japanese irezumi, flash sheet layouts
- **Target:** 25-45, alt/punk/metal, tattoo culture
- **POD execution:** Flash sheet layouts (multiple small designs on one tee) trending hard 2025-2026. Distressed vintage print adds authenticity
- **420Blazin products:** Skull & roses tees, long sleeve tattoo edition, flash sheet stickers

### F. Anime / Manga Crossover
- **Visual:** Anime-style characters with cannabis elements, Japanese katakana text, vaporwave colors
- **Target:** 18-30, Gen Z, internet-native
- **POD execution:** Strong on tees and stickers. Create ORIGINAL characters in anime style. Back prints with manga panel layouts
- **420Blazin products:** Anime character series, Japanese text variants

## 10. Emerging Trends (2025-2026)

### Y2K Revival
Chrome text, butterfly motifs, frosted/translucent effects, baby blue/pink/silver. Chrome cannabis leaf + Y2K fonts. Works on lighter garments. **Target:** Gen Z nostalgic for an era they didn't experience.

### Vaporwave / Synthwave
Neon pink/cyan/purple gradients, Greek statues, sunset grids, Japanese text, 80s computer aesthetic. Established Etsy market for "Weed Vaporwave." Neon-on-dark prints beautifully on black. **Target:** 20-35 internet/retro-gaming culture.

### Cottagecore Cannabis
Soft pastels, wildflowers, botanical illustration, vintage seed-packet design. Cannabis as just another plant in a beautiful garden. **MASSIVELY UNDERSERVED** in cannabis merch. Works on totes, soft tees, mugs. **Target:** Women 22-35, Pinterest-driven.

### Mushroom / Psychedelic Crossover
Combined cannabis + psilocybin imagery, sacred geometry, mycelium networks, third-eye motifs. Growing as psychedelic decriminalization expands. Deep purples, cosmic blues, gold. **Target:** 22-40 psychonaut, microdosing culture.

### Sacred Geometry / Spiritual
Flower of Life, Sri Yantra, Fibonacci spirals, mandala structures, chakra imagery. Cannabis leaf constructed from geometric shapes. Gold line work on black. **Target:** 25-40 yoga/meditation/tattoo.

### AI-Generated Art (The Double Edge)
Hyperdetailed, surreal compositions. AI dramatically lowers design barriers but risks saturation. **Critical:** Gen Z is developing AI-detection sensitivity — obviously AI designs face backlash. The differentiator is prompt engineering + human post-processing refinement. Hand-drawn imperfection is increasingly valued as counterpoint.

### The Hybrid Workflow (Recommended)

This is the winning formula:
1. **AI generates artwork** (60-70% of design) — illustration, pattern, imagery
2. **Human adds typography** (20-25%) — brand name, slogans in Illustrator/Kittl
3. **Human does composition** (10-15%) — arranging, spacing, print-area fit
4. **Human does color correction** — print-accurate colors, consistent palette

This also strengthens copyright position (substantial human creative expression added to AI base).

### Tie-Dye Transparency Technique

AI generators cannot produce true transparent PNGs. For tie-dye and colored garments:

**Step 1:** Generate white-only elements on solid black background
```
ALL WHITE ELEMENTS ONLY on solid pure black background #000000,
no color except white and light gray, high contrast
```

**Step 2:** Convert black to transparent via grayscale alpha
```bash
/opt/homebrew/bin/magick input.png -colorspace Gray mask.png
/opt/homebrew/bin/magick -size 1024x1024 xc:"srgba(255,255,255,0)" canvas.png
/opt/homebrew/bin/magick canvas.png mask.png -compose CopyOpacity -composite PNG32:output.png
```

**Step 3:** Upload via base64 (Printify upload-by-URL only works for public images)
```bash
BASE64=$(base64 -i output.png)
# Write to JSON file to avoid shell arg limits
echo "{\"file_name\":\"design.png\",\"contents\":\"$BASE64\"}" > /tmp/payload.json
curl -s -X POST "https://api.printify.com/v1/uploads/images.json" \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer $PRINTIFY_TOKEN" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" -d @/tmp/payload.json
```

## 14. Print-on-Demand Technical Specs

### Resolution Requirements

| Product | Min DPI | Recommended | Pixel Size |
|---------|---------|-------------|------------|
| T-shirts (DTG) | 150 | 300 | 4500x5400 px |
| AOP apparel | 150 | 200+ | 4500x5400 px |
| Stickers | 300 | 300 | varies |
| Posters | 300 | 300 | 3600x5400 px |

**The AI gap:** Most AI generates 1024-2048px. A 2048px image at 300 DPI = ~6.8 inches. Standard tee print = 14x16 inches = 4200x4800px. **Always upscale.**

### Color Space

- **Submit in sRGB** (sRGB IEC61966-2.1). All POD providers accept sRGB and convert internally
- Never pre-convert to CMYK — causes double-conversion degradation
- Cannabis green (#00FF00 pure green) shifts noticeably in CMYK — use warmer, slightly desaturated greens
- **Order physical samples** before committing to a product line

### DTG vs. Sublimation vs. DTF

| Aspect | DTG | Sublimation (AOP) | DTF |
|--------|-----|-------------------|-----|
| Best fabric | Cotton | Polyester only | Any |
| File format | PNG (transparent) | PNG/JPEG (full coverage) | PNG (transparent) |
| White ink | Auto white base on dark | No white ink | Always white base |
| Dark garments | Yes | No (polyester, usually white) | Yes |
| Hand feel | Ink absorbed | No feel (dye in fabric) | Slight film |
| Durability | Good (50+ washes) | Excellent (permanent) | Good (50+ washes) |

### Canvas Size Reference

| Product | Canvas (px) | Aspect | Notes |
|---------|-------------|--------|-------|
| T-shirt front | 4500x5100 | ~0.88:1 | Standard DTG, portrait |
| T-shirt (AI gen) | 1024x1024 | 1:1 | Krea generates 1024, works fine |
| Hat leather patch | 3000x1200 | 2.5:1 | Generate at 1024x416 |
| Sticker | 1024x1024 | 1:1 | Square for most |
| Hoodie embroidery | 1024x1024 | 1:1 | Small print area, keep simple |
| AOP front | 4500x5100 | ~0.88:1 | Full garment coverage |
| AOP sleeve | 3398x3863 | ~0.88:1 | Vertical flow, top-to-bottom |


## 16. Legal Considerations

### AI-Generated Art Copyright (2025-2026)

- **Purely AI-generated works cannot be copyrighted** (US Supreme Court denied cert in *Thaler v. Perlmutter*, March 2026)
- **Hybrid workflow strengthens copyright** — substantial human modification (compositing, typography, recoloring) creates copyrightable derivative
- **Document your human contribution** — keep layer files, editing records
- **Practical impact:** Competitors can freely copy unmodified AI output. Your protection = brand recognition + speed + human-modified elements

### Cannabis Imagery on Platforms

| Platform | Cannabis Merch Policy |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Printify** | Allowed. Features 420-themed collections on their blog |
| **Shopify** | Allowed for merchandise. Restrictions on actual cannabis/CBD products |
| **Etsy** | Allowed for apparel/art. Prohibits paraphernalia |
| **Stripe/PayPal** | Generally fine for merch with cannabis imagery (not actual cannabis) |
| **Google/Meta/TikTok Ads** | Prohibit cannabis advertising. Use "lifestyle apparel" framing. Organic social + SEO are primary channels |

### Trademark

- **"420"** is generic/descriptive — weak trademark protection
- **Cannabis leaf icon** is generic and unprotectable alone
- **"420Blazin"** as a distinctive brand name CAN be federally registered for apparel/merch (clothing is legal)
- Protect the original brand name and logo via federal trademark (apparel goods class)
- Use copyright on modified/original designs

## 17. What Makes People Actually Buy

### Purchase Triggers (Ranked by Impact)

**1. Identity Signaling** (Strongest)
Cannabis merch is fundamentally about identity. "The weed t-shirt is a symbol of identity and community." People wear it to display affiliation, signal to other consumers, support normalization, and express self-image.

**2. "Conversation Starter" Factor**
Designs that provoke reactions — laughs, compliments, questions — have dramatically higher conversion than generic designs. The best-performing designs make the wearer feel clever or funny.

**3. Design Quality Perception**
The gap between "cheap-looking" and "premium" is the single biggest conversion factor after price.
- **Cheap signals:** Clip-art leaf, basic green-on-white, low-res, Comic Sans, one-color
- **Premium signals:** Multi-color detailed illustration, professional typography, AOP, unusual palettes, artistic interpretation, vintage distressed effects

**4. Social Proof / Influencer Validation**
Cannabis influencers are "full media brands" — followers actively buy into their product ecosystems. The path: see on social → visit brand → check reviews/followers → purchase.

**5. Scarcity / FOMO**
60% of consumers make FOMO-driven purchases within 24 hours. 45% attracted to "difficult to obtain" products. Limited editions, countdown timers, "only X left" messaging.

**6. Gift-Giving Occasions**
Calendar-driven spikes: 420 Day (51% boost), Christmas season (46% of top sales days), birthdays, Valentine's, Mother's/Father's Day.

**7. Price-to-Quality Ratio**
The customer mentally compares: Redbubble ($20-25, no brand) vs. Cookies ($33-85, established) vs. random Etsy ($18-28, variable). 420Blazin needs to visually communicate "$40 quality at $25-30 pricing" through mockup presentation and design quality.
